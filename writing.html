Section I Portfolio Introduction / “About Me”

April 26, 2016

Technical Design

As a designer, one must have a need to educate, inspire, and encourage users to seek out additional experiences that help them grow in a form that makes sense. Interactive experiences can take on many forms, and creating iterations of them that cater to specific audiences in tactile ways can cause play that can connect with players on deep emotional and psychological levels. Familiarizing players with the interactive experience using well built mechanics and purposeful world building can introduce them to methods of creating and feeling that they can take into their work outside of the interactive experience.

February 29, 2016

Design Philosophy V1

I want to design to inspire others. Games as a medium allow players to experience exactly what the designer creates for them. This means that games can be used for fun, solving problems, addressing issues, or teaching. My knowledge of game design and computer science help me create what I think other people with the same common interests as me would want. Studying multiple subjects allows me to appeal to a broader audience, thus making my games more effective. I am designing for the individual who enjoys taking apart things to find out how they work. I create complex game systems that guide the player in having an enriching experience that they can grow from. The kinds of games I make are replayable and contain lots of content for added depth. Writing wise, I tend to steer towards very specific story archetypes. Heroes conquering inner demons, saving the land, or fighting injustice usually get written about in my games. The stories have a lot of further depth that is presented in the mechanics of the games. I consume a lot of contemporary media. My ideal reading material is usually an opinion piece on a book, movie, or other creative work. I enjoy seeing how other people felt about specific things and why they felt those ways. It helps me get a grasp of how other perspectives are viewing the same things I consume. When I come around to going through something I’ve already read someone's opinion about, knowing the intended effect greatly improves the experience for me.

Section III Game Narrative Review

I chose Stacking as my game to review because of it's unusual setting and unique gameplay. I discovered in the process that when you approach a game from a critical standpoint, the rose tinted goggles come off. You're able to see a lot more of the games flaws. But you're also able to greater understand and appreciate the work that went into the finished product. Thinking about Stacking's narrative enabled me to think of more of the process behind presenting narrative to the player. Trees for decisions the player makes and narrative become more obvious the more you go through and check how each player choice affects the game world. My goal post graduation is to become a Technical Designer. I think I can better achieve that goal by applying the thinking of dialogue being a large branching tree. Understanding the systems behind how things trigger certain events in the game can help me create systems better as well as design them more effectively.

Section IV Collaborative Story Project

I played a sizeable role in putting together the initial concept for the story. We wanted to create a story that had a character we could really write about.

Working in a group was okay. There was a lot of miscommunication in between group members at points. A lot of work that we turned in was not of the best quality due to group members not leaving others enough time to edit.

If I were to apply lessons to working on a team professionally. I would take a more formal stance on group work. Being able to hold ourselves accountable would have let us do more for our project.

Manburger is an interactive choose your own adventure where you play as Jeffrey, a psychopathic food truck operator, as he discovers how to run a business and run away from the authorities. Manburger will feature scenarios where the player will be tested to make difficult choices based on how they can escape the authorities.

The problem in game writing that I think our story attempts to fix is the writing for games with Text-based systems. Text based games often become predictable and banal. If a designer were to create a game with a circular progression around a certain element of the story. The game would constantly change and the player would be kept on their toes as they go through the story. We fix this by keeping choices in the game set to random, while leaving everything else to be a static hard coded variable.
