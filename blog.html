AVT 180 Blog

The beginning

As a student in AVT 180 at George Mason University, it is required of me to submit blog posts detailing my thoughts and analyses of the readings we are presented by our preofessor in the course. For every blog post on this page, I will try and write a bit about how I feel towards the authors writing as well as how it may effect me as an artist.



Post 1

http://www.berk-edu.com/VisualStudies/readingList/06b_benjamin-work%20of%20art%20in%20the%20age%20of%20mechanical%20reproduction.pdf

This source raises some interesting ideas about the authenticity possessed by original works of art, and the ways in which reproduction alters this authenticity. The author argues that through reproduction, original pieces of art lose their “aura”, or the unique presence they once had. This is an important point to consider, especially in an age when mass reproduction of art has become the norm. However, I do not know if I agree that the reproduction of artwork constitutes a loss of aura. Instead, I would say it leads more to an expanding of its aura, as it is now able to be felt by more people. While it is true that many people may not be experiencing it in its original context, this simply means that it is taking on new meanings as it is viewed in these different contexts. This is something I can keep in mind as people view my art and reproductions of it.



Post 2

http://tmducharme.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/ONE-PAGE-Steal-Like-an-Artist.pdf

This source presents numerous pieces of advice for aspiring artists. I felt that the advice provided here was very valuable, and I plan on utilizing much of it. In particular, the parts which read “You’re ready. Start making stuff” and “Make stuff every day. Fail. Get better” spoke to me. It is entirely too easy for ideas of not being ready/good enough and fear of failure to prevent an artist from doing work. However, unless you can push past these ideas and create something, regardless of how “good” it is, then you will never grow as an artist. In the future I will try to remind myself of this when I am hesitant to start working on a new project.



Post 3

http://www.after1968.org/app/webroot/uploads/SimulacraSimulation.pdf

This source discusses the nature of reality versus simulations. The author argues that while in the past creations were always reflections or depictions of what was real, simulations invert this, as they depict a sort different sort of realness that does not have origins in reality as we know it. I had not thought of simulation in these terms before, so this was interesting to me. The ideas in this source are particularly relevant towards art today, as simulation technology is progressing at a rapid rate. Within my own field of video game design, virtual reality is currently emerging as an increasingly accessible feature, so this concept of simulation as a new form of reality will likely have great impact on my future work.



Post 4

http://www.nytimes.com/1992/07/05/arts/art-view-virtual-reality-is-it-art-yet.html?pagewanted=all

This article discusses the emergence of virtual reality as an art form. Because this article was written in 1992, it was interesting reading it from a modern perspective, when virtual reality is beginning to become much more commonplace and is being utilized for both artistic and entertainment purposes. In many ways, virtual reality is new medium in and of itself, and as such its potential has yet to be fully explored, so this source’s discussion of virtual reality as art is still valuable today. As someone going into game design, it is likely that I will be working with virtual reality in the future, and when I do I will consider the ways in which it can utilized as an artistic medium.



Post 5

http://www.art21.org/artists

This source provides biographical information about many modern artists. Looking through the website, I found information on both artists I had heard of and artists I had not heard of, so I feel that this site could be valuable in expanding my artistic knowledge. As mentioned in one of the other sources, it is beneficial for artists to identify other artists they admire, and then identify who they admired, and so on. In this way, you can expand your influences and find new sources of inspiration for your own art.



Post 6

http://www.random-magazine.net/

This source has many articles about different types of digital art, such as videos, installations, music, and games. This website exposed me to many forms of art that I had not previously encountered, and as such made me think about just how broad the definition of art can be. By broadening my definition of art, this website can help me to broaden the scope of my own artwork. Aspiring artists can use websites such as this one to draw inspiration from the work of other artists who are using new mediums and creating new and genre-defying art.



Post 7

http://atc.berkeley.edu/201/readings/New%20Media%20Art%20-%20Introduction%20-%20Mark%20Tribe%20-%20Brown%20University%20Wiki.pdf

When being presented with the definitions and terms that Berkeley’s paper puts forth, it’s very easy to gain a better understanding of the legitimacy of new media art in relation to more traditional, forms like fine art. New Media as is encapsulates a good amount of what is available to those perusing content in media in all forms today. It’s interesting seeing the themes and how inspirations for new media came together to form what it has become today. Being able to appropriate other forms of content and make it your own is what really speaks out to me, and I believe that it is the strongest point of expression for those who choose to work in New Media. The institution is strongest when the audience becomes actively invested in it.



Post 8

http://whitney.org/

This source has many different forms of media available on it. From a cursory glance at the website, I can tell that it is very driven by its individual works gaining traction. A user wouldn’t get much information just from looking at the website, but they’re free to explore each little part of the museum piece by piece. The Whitney Museum seems like the perfect bastion of mixed medias for the burgeoning artist in a place like New York, which is full of different artists of different mediums trying to go their own way. One of my favorite parts of the site came from the “learning” section. Although not counting as new media itself, the way the Whitney Museum presents its learning section seems pretty good, especially for those looking to get into art.
