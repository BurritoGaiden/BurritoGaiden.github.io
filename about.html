About Me

Hello! My name is Luis Brito, and this is my Game Design Portfolio!
I'm a designer, but I have always held onto the idea that the more you understand all sides of a process, the better you'll be able to design.
I stick to this philosophy by being not only a designer, but also a programmer.

I study Computer Science and Game Design at George Mason University, where I am currently finishing my junior year.

I am the Chair for IGDA GMU, Researcher for Mason Gear, and am Design Director for GADIG. With IGDA GMU, I lead students interested in careers in game design by organizing events in which they can network, gain feedback from their peers, tour studios, interview industry professionals, and acquire internships. As a Researcher for Mason GEAR I study the emotional and physiological reactions new and recurring players experience while interacting with a VR environment. And as the Design Director for GADIG, I lead a team of 25 game design students as we design fun, rich games that accurately showcase each individual students' abilities.

Throughout the academic year I set aside enough time between my studies to work part-time as a programmer and designer for Little Arms Studios, a local studio founded by GMU graduates several years ago, on their multiple game and simulation projects.

For the past year I have been working with a small team of indie developers as a programmer and designer to release "Blood Alloy" for Suppressive Fire Games. We are set to release February 2016.

Aside from everything else, I do contract work usually involving helping developers brainstorm, document, and write code as well as feedback for their games.

Check out the games I've worked on by clicking on the Projects tab!
I have shipped two titles so far and am always working on something new. Check this site or my Twitter for updates on what I'm currently working on.
