GAME 332 RS Final Portfolio Outline

The purpose of preparing the portfolio is for you to reflect on your work this semester and present that work to others in the community. Construct the identity you hope to portray through the presentation of your work, placing it in the context of your goals and voice as a designer/creator. The various sections below could be tabs on your website, but you can also include them on a continuous single page.

In addition to including the work you created this semester, please open each section with a brief reflection that contextualizes the work. Some questions/prompts are below to guide you.

Suggested Portfolio Outline:

Section I Portfolio Introduction / “About Me”

April 26, 2016 Technical Design As a designer, one must have a need to educate, inspire, and encourage users to seek out additional experiences that help them grow in a form that makes sense. Interactive experiences can take on many forms, and creating iterations of them that cater to specific audiences in tactile ways can cause play that can connect with players on deep emotional and psychological levels. Familiarizing players with the interactive experience using well built mechanics and purposeful world building can introduce them to methods of creating and feeling that they can take into their work outside of the interactive experience. February 29, 2016 Design Philosophy V1 I want to design to inspire others. Games as a medium allow players to experience exactly what the designer creates for them. This means that games can be used for fun, solving problems, addressing issues, or teaching. My knowledge of game design and computer science help me create what I think other people with the same common interests as me would want. Studying multiple subjects allows me to appeal to a broader audience, thus making my games more effective. I am designing for the individual who enjoys taking apart things to find out how they work. I create complex game systems that guide the player in having an enriching experience that they can grow from. The kinds of games I make are replayable and contain lots of content for added depth. Writing wise, I tend to steer towards very specific story archetypes. Heroes conquering inner demons, saving the land, or fighting injustice usually get written about in my games. The stories have a lot of further depth that is presented in the mechanics of the games. I consume a lot of contemporary media. My ideal reading material is usually an opinion piece on a book, movie, or other creative work. I enjoy seeing how other people felt about specific things and why they felt those ways. It helps me get a grasp of how other perspectives are viewing the same things I consume. When I come around to going through something I’ve already read someone's opinion about, knowing the intended effect greatly improves the experience for me.
